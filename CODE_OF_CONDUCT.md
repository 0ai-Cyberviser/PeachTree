# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. PeachTree is dedicated to fostering an open, inclusive, and respectful environment for developers, contributors, users, and maintainers.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Respectful communication** — Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive feedback** — Offer helpful criticism and accept it graciously
- **Collaborative approach** — Work together toward common goals
- **Accountability** — Take responsibility for mistakes and learn from them
- **Inclusive attitude** — Be welcoming to people of all backgrounds
- **Focus on code** — Discuss ideas and code, not personal attributes
- **Open-mindedness** — Consider different perspectives and experiences

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** — Offensive comments related to personal characteristics
- **Trolling** — Deliberate provocation or inflammatory comments
- **Discrimination** — Treating people unfairly based on background
- **Unwelcome advances** — Persistent inappropriate contact
- **Doxxing** — Publishing private information without consent
- **Threats** — Any form of intimidation or threats
- **Spam** — Excessive off-topic posts or promotion

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (repositories, issues, discussions, wiki)
- Official events and conferences
- Project communication channels
- Representing PeachTree in public forums
- Private communications related to project work

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Document the incident** — Note details, timestamps, and context
2. **Report to maintainers** — Email conduct@cyberviser.io
3. **Include evidence** — Screenshots, links, or message excerpts (when safe)
4. **Remain confidential** — Reports are kept private and investigated discreetly

Include in your report:

- Description of the incident
- Who was involved
- When and where it occurred
- How it affected you or others
- Any previous incidents or patterns

### Response

- **Acknowledgment** — Within 24 hours
- **Investigation** — Within 72 hours
- **Action** — Appropriate response determined by maintainers
- **Follow-up** — Communication about resolution

### Consequences

Depending on severity, enforcement may include:

1. **Warning** — Private communication about unacceptable behavior
2. **Temporary suspension** — Access revoked for 24-48 hours
3. **Permanent ban** — Removal from project and communities
4. **Escalation** — Report to appropriate authorities if necessary

## Accountability

Maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate action against violations
- Being fair and consistent in enforcement
- Protecting the privacy of reporters
- Addressing violations promptly
